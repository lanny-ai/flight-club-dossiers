Section 05 // Vulnerabilities

SEVEN KILL SHOTS

01

DC IS THEIR SIDE HUSTLE

CMC rigid inclusions are Menard's proprietary flagship product, not dynamic compaction. When their sales team fields a call, they have a financial incentive to steer prospects toward CMC (proprietary, higher margin, lock-in). DC is generic with no lock-in. It will always get less corporate attention, less marketing spend, and less priority in their pipeline.

Exploit

"We don't have 10 techniques competing for our attention. We have one. When you call us, you talk to people who've done nothing but DC for 33 years. When you call Menard, you talk to a salesperson deciding whether to pitch you DC or sell you something more profitable for them."

02

CORPORATE OVERHEAD = HIGHER PRICES

200 to 400 employees. 15+ offices. French parent company. Layers of management: Chairman, CEO, CFO, multiple SVPs, VPs, Directors. All of it baked into their bids. On a pure DC project, Densification can undercut them by 10-20% while maintaining healthy margins.

Exploit

Every dollar of corporate overhead is a dollar you don't carry. On a $500K project, that's $50K to $100K in difference. Your lean structure is a pricing weapon they cannot match without bleeding margin.

03

DIGESTING TWO ACQUISITIONS

Farrell Design-Build (2021) and Earth Tech (2022) were just rebranded under the Menard name as of January 2026. Cultural integration takes 2 to 3 years minimum. They are in the messy middle right now. Senior management attention is split. Systems are being unified. Employees are uncertain about roles and culture.

Exploit

While Menard is focused inward on integration headaches, Densification focuses outward on clients. Their distraction is your opportunity.

04

DC EXPERTISE IS TWO PEOPLE DEEP

Menard's entire DC capability rests on Woods and Shaffer. Their existing staff is trained in CMC and vibro techniques, not dynamic compaction. If either hire leaves, gets promoted into a corporate role, or underperforms, the DC division crumbles. Meanwhile, Densification still has Joe (33 years), Sam (9 years), Robert Fulton (29 years), and the field crews who actually do the work.

Exploit

Every significant decision flows through Soletanche Freyssinet to VINCI S.A. Pricing approvals, capital investments, equipment purchases, new market entries: all require corporate approval chains across time zones and management layers. Joe Drumheller says yes on the spot.
