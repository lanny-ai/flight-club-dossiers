PART 4: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

Audio-Technica vs. Sennheiser

Sennheiser's Position: - In 2021, Sennheiser sold its consumer audio division to Sonova (hearing aid maker), retaining only professional audio. This was a major strategic refocus. - Professional Sennheiser is now fully enterprise-focused: TeamConnect conferencing systems, Evolution Wireless digital, MKH studio mics, AMBEO spatial audio. - Very strong relationships with corporate AV integrators and enterprise IT procurement. - Strong brand premium — Sennheiser still commands significant price premium in the corporate conferencing space. - UK team is well-resourced and has deep relationships with the integration channel.

Where A-T Wins vs. Sennheiser: - Value/quality ratio — A-T wireless and conferencing systems deliver comparable quality at lower price points - Live sound heritage — stronger specification preference among live event professionals - Turntable and phono heritage drives brand affinity among studio engineers who then specify A-T microphones - Distribution model — A-T's multi-brand portfolio means dealers can get a complete solution from one relationship - Dante integration (ATND series) is competitive with Sennheiser's own networked audio offerings

Where A-T is Vulnerable vs. Sennheiser: - Sennheiser TeamConnect Ceiling 2 (TCC2) is a dominant product in corporate conferencing — broadly specified and difficult to displace - Sennheiser's enterprise sales relationships are more established in IT-led procurement (where decisions go to Sennheiser and Shure first) - Brand perception gap — Sennheiser still carries more corporate prestige in Europe - Sennheiser's evolution wireless digital (EW-D) is widely loved by live sound professionals

Audio-Technica vs. Shure

Shure's Position: - The dominant force in wireless microphones globally, particularly in the corporate/conferencing segment - Microflex Advance (MXA) series — ceiling arrays, table arrays — are the default specification for premium conference rooms - IntelliMix Room (IMX Room) DSP software creates a complete software-based conferencing solution - Axient Digital wireless is the standard for premium live event and broadcast production - Shure SystemOn remote management software has become a standard for enterprise AV system management - Strong relationships with Crestron, Extron, Microsoft, and Cisco — creating integrated bundle specifications

Where A-T Wins vs. Shure: - Mid-market value — A-T wireless systems (ATW-3000/4000 series) offer strong performance at competitive price points - ATUC conferencing systems compete well in the discussion/conferencing segment, particularly for government and education - A-T's Dante-enabled ATND boundary and gooseneck mics compete directly with Shure's MXA910/920 at lower price points - Distribution portfolio strength — A-T can offer a complete installed sound solution (mics + mixing + speakers + acoustics) that Shure cannot - Live sound: Professional engineers who use A-T for broadcast/recording often carry that brand preference into live sound

Where A-T is Vulnerable vs. Shure: - Shure MXA ceiling array products are deeply entrenched in the premium corporate market — very difficult to displace on spec - Shure's software ecosystem (IntelliMix Room, SystemOn) creates switching costs that pure hardware comparison doesn't capture - Shure's relationship with major UC platform vendors (Microsoft Teams, Zoom, Google Meet) gives them automatic certification advantages in the hybrid meeting space - In the premium live sound segment, Axient Digital is the gold standard and A-T's equivalent doesn't command the same prestige - Shure's US-centric customer base means European pricing can be aggressive when they're defending accounts

Other Competitive Threats

DPA Microphones - Danish ultra-premium microphone brand (now owned by Sennheiser) - Dominant in broadcast lavalier microphones — A-T's ATM350 series competes here - Very high price point creates natural market segmentation — A-T often wins on value
